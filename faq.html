Getting Started

An eSIM is a digital SIM built directly into your phone during manufacturing. Instead of inserting a physical plastic card, you scan a QR code or enter an activation code to install a data plan. DataSafari emails you the QR instantly after purchase — no store visit, no waiting, no plastic.
Carrier roaming charges $15–$45 per day for data in most countries. DataSafari starts from $3 for 7 days. We buy data wholesale from local networks in 200+ countries and pass the savings on to you. Same networks, same speeds — up to 85% less cost.
No app is required. The QR code works directly through your phone's built-in Settings. We do offer a companion app for managing plans and top-ups, but it's entirely optional. The eSIM works the same either way.

Installation

Open Settings → Mobile Data (or Cellular) → Add eSIM → Use QR Code. Point your camera at the QR from your DataSafari email. Tap Continue, name your plan (e.g. "DataSafari"), set it as your data line, and enable Data Roaming. Takes under 2 minutes.
Open Settings → Connections → SIM Card Manager → Add eSIM. Tap "Scan QR code from carrier" and point at your QR email. On Pixel devices, go to Settings → Network & Internet → SIMs → Add SIM. Follow the prompts, set DataSafari as the default data line, and enable Data Roaming.
Every email includes a manual activation code (SM-DP+ address + activation code) you can type in instead. Go to the same Add eSIM screen and choose "Enter details manually". You can also contact our 24/7 support and we'll resend the QR instantly.
Always install before you travel, while you have reliable Wi-Fi at home. The profile installs and sits dormant. Validity only begins when your phone first connects to a local network in your destination. You won't lose any plan days by installing early.
Each eSIM plan is tied to one device. If you need connectivity on multiple devices (phone + tablet), you'll need a separate plan for each. Most plans include hotspot tethering, so you can share your phone's data connection to other devices.

Compatibility

Dial *#06# on your phone. If an EID number appears in the result, your device is eSIM-capable. You can also check Settings → Mobile Data or SIM Manager — if you see "Add eSIM" or similar, you're compatible. Most smartphones released from 2018 onwards support eSIM.
Absolutely. DataSafari runs on your eSIM slot for data while your physical SIM continues to handle calls and texts from your regular number. Both work simultaneously. You can call and text on your home number while browsing on DataSafari data.
If your phone is carrier-locked, the eSIM slot may be restricted to your home carrier's profiles only. DataSafari is unlikely to activate on a locked device. Contact your carrier to unlock — most carriers offer free unlocks after your contract period ends.
Yes, for eSIM-capable tablets. Compatible iPads include iPad Pro 2018+, iPad Air 2019+, iPad mini 5th gen+, and iPad 7th gen+. Installation steps are identical — go to Settings → Mobile Data → Add eSIM and scan the QR code.
If your laptop has eSIM hardware (some Windows devices and certain MacBooks), yes. Otherwise use the hotspot feature from your phone after installing DataSafari — enable Personal Hotspot (iPhone) or Mobile Hotspot (Android) and connect your laptop via Wi-Fi.

Troubleshooting

Check these in order: 1) Data Roaming is ON for the DataSafari eSIM line (not just your home SIM). 2) DataSafari is set as the default for mobile data. 3) Toggle Airplane Mode on and off and wait 30 seconds. 4) Manually select a local network under Mobile Data → Network Selection.
